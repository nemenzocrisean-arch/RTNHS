About Ramon Torres National High School

History

Ramon Torres National High School (RTNHS) was established in 1947, originally named Bago Junior High School. Over the years, it underwent several name changes before being renamed Ramon Torres Memorial High School in 1977, honoring former Senator Ramon Torres, the school’s chief founder. In 1983, Batas Pambansa Blg. 411 officially converted it into a national high school, broadening its academic and vocational programs.

Mission & Vision

Mission: To provide quality education and holistic development to all students, empowering them to become responsible citizens of the community.

Vision: To become a leading educational institution recognized for academic excellence, extracurricular achievements, and community involvement.

Campus & Facilities

The main campus of RTNHS is located on General Luna Street, Barangay Balingasag, Bago City. It features multiple classroom buildings, administrative offices, a covered court, an e-library, technical vocational laboratories, and other modern facilities designed to enhance student learning.

Academic Programs

RTNHS offers Junior High School (Grades 7–10) following the K to 12 curriculum and Senior High School (Grades 11–12) with Academic Tracks including STEM, ABM, HUMSS, and GAS, as well as Technical-Vocational-Livelihood (TVL) tracks like Cookery, Tailoring, Electrical, and Welding.

Achievements

RTNHS students have earned recognition in regional competitions such as robotics contests and sports tournaments. The school emphasizes both academic excellence and extracurricular involvement to foster well-rounded learners.

Community Involvement

The school actively engages with the local community through civic programs, youth leadership activities, and alumni events. It serves as a hub for both education and community development in Bago City.
